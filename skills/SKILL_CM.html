Contact Management for beginners

What is Contact Management (CM) or Personal Information Manager (PIM)

We all make contacts as we live our lives; relatives, friends, neighbors, work associates, customers.

Let's define contact information as a piece of information that uniquely identifies that person for you but the identification method is owned by someone else, a third party.

The original contact method was by US land mail address.

100 years ago telephone number contact was added.

In the last 20 years, email, cell phone, instant messaging, public key encryption and more have been added as technology has changed.

Personal Information Manager adds other information about the person to the contact information - like maybe a spouse name, a child's name, a position in a company.
  • personal notes
  • task lists
  • calendar dates (birthday, anniversaries, appointments)
  • voicemail
  • project manangement
Today we will focus on the CM portion only, mainly:
  • US mail (home, buisness, other)
  • phone numbers: land (house, buisness), cell, fax, secondary
  • email (personal, buisness)
  • instant message agents (aol, yahoo, msn)
  • public key encryption

Taxonomy of CM methods

Everyone does CM by some method. We starts simple:

A piece of paper, a scratch by the wall phone.

An address book from office depot (often large first, small second).

We graduate to cell phones and company business databases and PDAs.

Business card scanners and ACT commercial software are the top of the line products today.

Outlook, the swiss army knife of Microsoft products is also commonly used.

Assets/liability questions:


What assets to consider when looking for your contact management choice:
  1. Is there a way to obsolete information?
  2. Are there ways to age information (keep old job and new job)
  3. Can contacts be categorized?
  4. What synchronization methods are offered?
What liabilities should you look out for?
  1. Is there a way to back up and restore contacts?
  2. Is it free or commercial?
  3. Is it compatible with your current contact system?
